Proving an Existential Statement.

To prove there exists \(x\in D\) such that \(P(x)\text{:}\)
  1. Produce a candidate. This means you state an \(x\in D\) which makes \(P(x)\) true. You may need to do work to find \(x\text{,}\) but that work is not part of the actual proof.
  2. Show the candidate works. In other words, show \(P(x)\) is true.
