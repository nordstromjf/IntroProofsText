In mathematics we might see statements such as “\(p\) is necessary for \(q\)” or “\(p\) is sufficient for \(q\)”. These are really conditional statements. In particular, \(p\) is sufficient for \(q\) means \(p\rightarrow q\text{;}\) and \(p\) is necessary for \(q\) means \(\neg p\rightarrow \neg q\text{,}\) which is equivalent to \(q\rightarrow p\text{.}\) So, a statement such as \(p\) is necessary and sufficient for \(q\) means \(p\leftrightarrow q\text{.}\)
