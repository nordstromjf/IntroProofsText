One last idea before beginning the proof, make sure you understand that every real number in \([0, 1]\) can be represented by a decimal of the form \(0.a_{i1}a_{i2}a_{i3}\ldots\) with infinitely many decimal places. This should be clear if the decimal is repeating or nonterminating, nonrepeating. If the decimal is terminating, we can just add infinitely many 0's.
